Privacy Policy — Site Companion Side Kick (SCSK)

Last updated: 15 December 2025

Summary Data we collect How we use data Sharing Security Your choices Contact
Plain-English summary:
  • SCSK is built for professional site workflows and is designed with privacy in mind.
  • We only use device permissions (camera, storage, location, microphone) when you enable features that need them.
  • We do not sell your personal information.
  • If syncing is enabled, your data may be stored and processed to provide cross-device access.

This Privacy Policy explains how Site Companion Side Kick (SCSK) (“we”, “us”, “our”) collects, uses, and protects information when you use the SCSK mobile application and related services (the “Service”).

1) Information we collect

Depending on what features you use, the Service may collect or process the following categories of information:

  • Account information: if you sign in, we may collect identifiers needed to authenticate you (for example, an email address or user ID provided by your sign-in method).
  • Project and job content you create: project names, job details, notes, checklists, defect items, timestamps, and trade assignments you enter.
  • Photos and files: photos you take inside the app, images you import, and documents you attach to jobs/projects (such as plans/SWMS where applicable).
  • Audio (optional): if you use voice notes or speech-to-text features, audio or transcripts may be processed to provide that functionality.
  • Location (optional): if you enable geofencing/location features, location data may be used to trigger prompts when you are near a saved site.
  • Device and diagnostics data: basic technical information (e.g., app version, device model, crash logs) to help improve stability and performance.
Beta note: SCSK is an active beta. We may add features over time that require new permissions or data processing. When this happens, we aim to update this policy and/or present in-app permission prompts so you can choose what you enable.

2) How we use information

We use information to:

  • Provide core app functionality (projects, jobs, photos, annotations, reporting).
  • Enable cross-device access and syncing when you choose to use syncing.
  • Generate documents and reports you request (including PDF exports).
  • Improve performance, reliability, and user experience (including bug fixes and feature improvements).
  • Provide support and respond to feedback requests.

3) Sharing of information

We may share information only in limited circumstances, such as:

  • With service providers: hosting, syncing, analytics, or crash reporting providers used to operate the Service.
  • When you choose to share: when you export or email reports, share PDFs, or send content to contacts/trades.
  • Legal requirements: if required to comply with applicable law or valid legal process.

We do not sell your personal information.

4) Security

We take reasonable measures to protect information. However, no system can be guaranteed 100% secure. You are responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of your device access and any sign-in credentials.

5) Your choices and controls

You can control your data and permissions in a few ways:

  • Permissions: you can enable/disable permissions (camera, microphone, location) in your device settings.
  • Exporting: you can export reports and store/share them as you choose.
  • Account/data requests: you may request assistance with account access or data questions by contacting us (see below).

6) Children’s privacy

SCSK is intended for professional, workplace use and is not directed to children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

7) Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated “Last updated” date.

8) Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or the Service, contact us:

  • Email: feedback@scsk.com
  • Website: https://sc-sk.com
Tip: If you’re contacting support about an issue, include your device model, Android version, and what you were doing when the issue happened.
